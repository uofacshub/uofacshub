Course Review Guidelines

Our course review system is designed to help students make informed decisions about their course selections. When submitting reviews:

  • Be honest and objective in your assessment
  • Focus on course content, teaching methods, and workload
  • Provide constructive feedback that could help improve the course
  • Avoid personal attacks on instructors or students
  • Share what you found valuable and what could be improved

All reviews are moderated to ensure they meet our community guidelines.

Start Your Own Club

Don't see a club that matches your interests? Start your own! The CS Hub provides support for new student organizations:

  1. Find at least 5 interested students
  2. Develop a club constitution and mission statement
  3. Submit your proposal through our club registration form
  4. We'll help with room bookings, promotion, and funding applications

New clubs receive mentorship from existing club leaders and access to our club resources.

About UAlberta CS Hub

Created by students, for students

Our Mission

The UAlberta CS Hub was founded in 2022 by a group of Computer Science students who saw the need for a centralized platform to support the growing CS community at the University of Alberta.

Our mission is to create a comprehensive resource that helps students navigate their academic journey, connect with peers, and access opportunities that will shape their future careers in technology.

We believe that by sharing knowledge and resources, we can build a stronger, more supportive community that empowers every student to succeed.

What We Do

The CS Hub is entirely student-run and maintained. We collaborate with faculty, industry partners, and student groups to provide:

  • Course reviews and academic planning resources
  • Centralized information about clubs and events
  • Study materials and past exams (ethically sourced)
  • Career development and internship opportunities
  • A platform for students to share knowledge and experiences
